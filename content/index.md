  This page establishes a comprehensive mapping between the rich interdisciplinary vocabulary of New Materialism, Posthumanism and a research focus on the entanglement of technology, writing, and pedagogy in higher education. In each case, it is intended to provide a foundation for extrapolating further theoretical development and guiding empirical inquiry.

  Diffraction & Reading Diffractively refers to both a theoretical concept and a methodological practice central to new materialist and postqualitative inquiry. Unlike reflection, which seeks to mirror or represent a pre-existing reality, diffraction attends to the patterns of difference, interference, and entanglement that emerge when phenomena inter-act . This method moves beyond simple cause-and-effect analysis, instead focusing on the complex, generative, and often unpredictable effects that arise from the entanglement of matter and meaning in educational assemblages. </details>
