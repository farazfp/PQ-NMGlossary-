### [[Agential Realism]]
- **Core Definition:** Agential Realism is an **onto-epistemological** framework developed by physicist and feminist theorist Karen Barad (2007). The term "onto-epistemology" signals a fundamental challenge to the traditional separation of ontology (the study of being) and epistemology (the study of knowing). For Barad, being and knowing are inseparable; we come to know the world through our direct material [[entanglement]] with it, and in doing so, we are co-constituting what the world is. It is a performative understanding of the world, where things do not pre-exist our engagement with them but rather emerge through specific material-discursive practices.
- **Rejection of Representationalism:**
	- Agential Realism moves away from the idea that knowledge, language, or theories are representations that either succeed or fail in mirroring an independent, pre-existing reality. Instead, our theories and inquiries are understood as material practices that actively participate in producing the reality they describe.
- **Key Concepts within Agential Realism:**
	- **[[Intra-action]]:** This is perhaps the most crucial concept. It replaces the traditional notion of "interaction."
		- *Interaction* assumes that pre-existing, independent entities meet and have an effect on one another.
		- *Intra-action* posits that entities do not pre-exist their relating. Rather, they emerge *through* their relationality. It is through the process of intra-action that the boundaries and properties of "things" are delineated.
	- **[[Phenomena]]:** The primary unit of reality is not the independent object but the **phenomenon**, which is the ontological inseparability of intra-acting "agencies." A phenomenon includes the entire material arrangement, meaning the "observer" and the "observed" are not separate but are co-constituted parts of the phenomenon.
	- **[[Agency]]:** Agency is not an attribute possessed by a subject (e.g., a human). It is a distributed "doing," an enactment. Agency is the capacity to make a difference, to cause change, and it is enacted by the entire [[apparatus]] of a phenomenon. In this sense, non-humans—technologies, discourses, objects—are agentive.
	- **[[Apparatus]]:** Apparatuses are the specific material-discursive practices that produce phenomena. They are not neutral instruments for observing the world but are the boundary-making practices through which a "cut" is made in the world, temporarily stabilizing certain entities and relations.
- **Connection to the Research Questions, [[Assemblages]], and [[Entanglements]]:**
	- **Reframing the Inquiry:** Agential Realism fundamentally reframes the research. The project is not studying the *impact of* [[Generative AI]] *on* instructors, as this would assume they are separate, pre-existing entities. Instead, the research investigates the **[[phenomenon]] of instructor-student-AI-pedagogy**. It asks: How is this phenomenon enacted? What realities are produced through this specific [[intra-action]]? What becomes possible and impossible within this [[entanglement]]?
	- **Grounding [[Assemblages]] and [[Entanglements]]:** This framework provides the philosophical grounding for using terms like [[assemblage]] and [[entanglement]].
		- The pedagogical [[assemblage]] (instructor, AI, policy, curriculum) is understood as an **[[apparatus]]** that enacts a specific educational reality.
		- The [[entanglement]] is not just a metaphor for complexity; it is the ontological condition of inseparability. The "instructor" is not a stable entity but is continuously being re-made through their [[intra-action]] with the AI, students, and institutional narratives.
	- **Analyzing [[Narrative]] and [[Anxiety]]:** From an agential realist perspective, [[narrative]] and [[anxiety]] are not immaterial.
		- [[Narrative]]s (e.g., "AI will make students lazy") are material-discursive practices that are part of the [[apparatus]]. They perform "agential cuts" that help to produce what counts as "real" learning versus "cheating."
		- [[Anxiety]] is not just a human [[emotion]] in response to technology; it is a material [[affect|force]] that is part of the [[phenomenon]] itself, a sign of the world reconfiguring.
- **Ethical and Methodological Implications:**
	- If inquiry is a form of [[intra-action]], then the research process itself is a worldly intervention. The researcher, their questions, and their methods are part of the [[apparatus]] they are studying. This creates a profound responsibility, as the research is not just describing the world but is actively participating in its becoming. This aligns with the [[ethics]] of [[Post-qualitative Inquiry]].
