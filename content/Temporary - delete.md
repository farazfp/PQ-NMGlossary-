### Agential Cut

> The enactment of a temporary, contingent separation within a phenomenon that makes some things matter and become intelligible while excluding others.

The agential cut is a core concept in **Karen Barad’s** agential realism, describing the material-discursive practice that resolves an inherent indeterminacy within a phenomenon. It is not a cut made _by_ an external agent onto the world, but a cut enacted _within_ the world’s ongoing intra-actions. This act of "cutting together-apart" simultaneously distinguishes and connects, producing a temporary local boundary between "subject" and "object" or "cause" and "effect" where none pre-exists. As the attached _Glossary for Doing Postqualitative... Research_ notes, this is a profound departure from a Cartesian cut, which assumes pre-existing, separate entities. An agential cut is always provisional, specific to the apparatus that enacts it, and carries ethical weight because it is through such cuts that different "marks on bodies" are produced and accountability is forged.

In postqualitative inquiry, the concept of the agential cut attunes the researcher to the performativity of their own methods and theoretical frameworks. The research apparatus—including interview questions, coding schemes, software, and conceptual lenses—is understood to enact agential cuts that make certain aspects of a phenomenon observable while rendering others invisible. This reframes objectivity not as a view from nowhere, but as a form of accountability for the cuts one’s research enacts and the exclusions they entail. It shifts the focus from discovering a pre-existing reality to tracing how realities are materialized through specific research practices.

This concept is crucial for analyzing how boundaries are drawn and redrawn in AI-entangled writing classrooms. For instance, an assignment prompt that forbids all use of an LLM enacts an agential cut that separates "student work" from "AI work," producing particular effects and accountabilities around plagiarism. Conversely, a prompt that requires students to document their intra-actions with an LLM enacts a different cut, materializing a different reality where human-AI collaboration is the object of study. The agential cut helps analyze how pedagogical artifacts (syllabi, rubrics) and platform designs (the user interface) performatively establish what counts as authorship, creativity, and legitimate academic practice.
